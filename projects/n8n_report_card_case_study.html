Automated Student Report Card Generation

An AI-driven n8n workflow that turns raw student data from spreadsheets into structured, narrative-style term reports — saving educators hours of manual work each term.

The business challenge

The end of term brings a heavy administrative burden: writing student report cards. It's time-consuming and repetitive, requiring teachers to synthesise months of data — assessments, homework logs, behavioural notes — into a coherent, constructive, personalised narrative for each student. That overhead directly impacts wellbeing and takes time away from teaching.

The solution: an AI report-writing assistant

I built an automated n8n workflow that acts as an AI teaching assistant. It ingests a simple log of student interactions and performance from Excel and uses an LLM to generate high-quality, structured reports for every student, ready for teacher review.

The automated workflow

  1. Data ingestion — connects to an Excel workbook containing a term's worth of student records: dates, record types, scores and qualitative comments.
  2. Data structuring — groups records by individual student, building a complete performance history per pupil.
  3. Dynamic AI prompting — constructs a tailored prompt per student instructing the model to produce a five-section report (overall summary, assessment performance, and more) from the data.
  4. Report generation — iterates through each student, generating a complete, well-written report card for export and final teacher approval.

Reduces the time spent writing report cards by over 80%, freeing teachers to focus on high-impact educational activities.

Key outcomes & impact

  • Drastic workload reduction — a fraction of the manual effort, with the teacher retaining final editorial control.
  • Quality & consistency — every report is well-structured and maintains a professional tone across the school.
  • Data-driven narratives — the AI synthesises all available data points into a true reflection of each student's term.
  • Improved morale — automating one of the most demanding admin tasks reduces burnout.
